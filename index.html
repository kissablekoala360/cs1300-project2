The Problem

Despite being a Fortune 500 company, John Deere has a website that displays a barrage of competing topics and lacks an understandable heirarchy. Let's take a look at John Deere's website, diagnose the problems, and give it a fresh look.

Let's Talk Design Issues

+

  • The website has three menus: the header menu, the list of vehicles and parts, and the secondary header menu under the main image.

  • With so many buttons, there is no clear call to action.

  • A user without knowledge of the site or a direct purpose may feel confused and purposeless on this website.

  • On mobile versions of the website, the second menu (list of machine image options) becomes the main focus of the landing page.

Thinking About Accessibility

+
  • Text on banner images are missing contrast.
  • Some form buttons are not clearly labeled.
  • There are redundant links on the front page.
  • Most images and links have alternative labeles.

Our New Look

Let's take our observations about the design issues and accessibility issues and create a new look for the website.
